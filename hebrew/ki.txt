Translate and explain: כִּ֣י
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Hebrew word כִּ֣י (ki) is a versatile conjunction that can be used in various
contexts.

Its meaning can range from "because," "for," "that," "when," or "if," depending
on the context in which it is used.

For example, in Exodus 20:11, כִּ֣י (ki) appears in the context of the Ten
Commandments, specifically related to the creation of the world and the
observance of the Sabbath:

Explain: כִּי (ki)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

(ki) is a conjunction in Hebrew that has several possible translations in
English, including

    "because"

    "for" 

    "since"

    "that"

It is commonly used to introduce a reason, explanation,
or cause for something that has been said or will be said.

For example, in the sentence

  "I didn't go to work today because I was sick,"

"because" is the English equivalent of כִּי.

In the sentence

  "He left early for the meeting since it was important,"

"since" is the English equivalent of כִּי.

It's important to note that כִּי can also be used to introduce
a clause that provides evidence or proof for a previous
statement, as in the sentence

  "I know he's coming, for he told me himself."

Here, "for" is the English equivalent of כִּי.

In Biblical Hebrew, כִּי is a common word used throughout the OT and appears in a
wide variety of contexts.
